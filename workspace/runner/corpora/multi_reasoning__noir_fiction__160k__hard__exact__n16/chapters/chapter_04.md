*"Met someone today. Can't write his name yet—feels too real if I write it down. But he's unlike anyone I've met before. Successful, confident, but there's something sad about him. Like he's carrying something that's too heavy. When he looks at me, I feel like he's really seeing me, not just looking at me. It's intoxicating and terrifying in equal measure."*

Over the next few weeks, the entries became more frequent and more intimate, though Catherine was careful never to write her lover's name. She referred to him as "H" in later entries.

*"H says I need to be more careful. Says the city isn't safe, especially for someone as beautiful and vulnerable as me. He's being protective, which I should probably find annoying, but instead I find it comforting. Is that weakness? Or is it just love? I can't tell anymore."*

*"H introduced me to the world of high finance today. Took me to a restaurant I'd never have afforded on my own. Introduced me to his business associates. I felt out of place, dressed too plainly, not sophisticated enough. But H made sure everyone knew I was with him, that I mattered to him. That made all the difference."*

Then, about three weeks ago, something shifted. The entries became worried.

*"H is different lately. Distracted. When I ask him what's wrong, he changes the subject or holds me without answering. I think something is happening in his business. Something serious. But he won't tell me what it is. He says it's better if I don't know. Says it's to protect me. But it feels like being kept in the dark. How can I help him if I don't understand what's happening?"*

*"Started noticing H having phone conversations late at night. He goes into the other room, speaks quietly, speaks urgently. When he comes back, he kisses my forehead and tells me he loves me, but there's distance in it. Like his mind is somewhere else, somewhere he's afraid to let me follow."*

The most recent entry was dated Monday—two days before she disappeared.

*"I told H today that I can't do this anymore—can't be with someone who won't let me in, won't share his burden. He became very still, very controlled. Said, 'You know too much already, Catherine. And loving me is already dangerous. Please don't ask for more.' Then he left. I don't know if he's coming back. I don't know if I want him to. Everything inside me is pulled in two directions. His full name is Harrison—Harrison Webb. I finally wrote it down. The world is real now."*

I closed the journal and sat on the bed, thinking. Catherine Halstead had been involved with someone—someone powerful, someone worried, someone who'd warned her about danger. And then she'd gone walking alone at night, and the next thing anyone knew, she was gone and there was a kidnapping note.

It was possible that her mysterious lover had taken her. It was possible that someone had taken her to get to him. It was possible that Catherine knew too much about something illegal or compromising, and this was a warning to keep quiet.

Or it was possible that I was overthinking this, and the answer was simpler than it appeared.

I was interrupted by a knock on the door. I turned to look at the exit, measuring distances, calculating whether I could get out without being seen. Then the door opened anyway.

The man in the doorway was police. I could tell by the way he carried the badge, the way his hand rested near his waist, the way he looked at me like I was a problem that needed solving. He was broad-shouldered, with dark hair starting to gray at the temples and a face that had seen worse things than it should have.

"Lieutenant Marcus Webb, Police Detective Bureau," he said, showing the badge. "And you are?"
