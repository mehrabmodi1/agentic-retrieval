"Is there anything else I should know?"

Victor stood, signaling that the interview was approaching its end. "There's a woman. I don't know her name, but she's connected to the operation somehow. Red hair, very beautiful, very cold. She came into the network about two years ago, and she's been Kaslan's personal acquisition specialist since then. Meaning she's the one who identifies valuable items and figures out how to extract them from current possession. She's also the one who eliminates complications."

"Complications like witnesses?"

"Complications like anyone who might talk. I only bring her up because my sources indicate she was seen meeting with Harvey about two weeks ago. And two weeks after a meeting with her, people tend to either disappear or die. The fact that Harvey disappeared suggests he may have negotiated with her. Or made a deal with her that required his complete withdrawal from the city."

"Do you have a name? A description?"

"The name she uses is Margot. That may or may not be real. The description I already gave you—red hair, beautiful, cold. But here's what matters: if she met with Harvey, it wasn't to kill him. It was to facilitate something. And that something was probably his exit from the Kaslan operation."

"What would that cost?"

"For Margot? Usually everything you have, and then your silence for the rest of your life." Victor moved toward the exit. "Your boy Harvey is either dead, or he's bought his freedom at the cost of his entire life. Either way, he's gone from this city. And Kaslan, despite losing the painting and losing Harvey, is still operating. Still acquiring. Still distributing. The Vesper painting was a loss—a significant one—but it's not enough to bring down the operation."

"What would bring it down?"

"Testimony," Victor said. "A full account of the network's operations, the names of the operatives, the locations of the storage facilities, the routes used for distribution. That would bring it down. And that's what Harvey had, or what Harvey could have provided. Which is almost certainly why, if he's still alive, he's living under a different name in a different city, terrified that one day Kaslan or Margot or Silas Gregg will find him and remind him why it's dangerous to develop a conscience."

He paused before leaving, then added one more detail. "The safe house where they keep acquisitions before moving them—it's somewhere in the residential district. Maybe Riverside, maybe Oak Hill. I heard Margot mention 'the place on Millbrook,' which might be a location. I never confirmed it, but if you're looking for physical evidence, that's where the pipeline begins."
