I sit down across from her. "I'm not looking to hurt anyone. I'm looking to understand what happened to a girl."

Sylvia puts down her pen. "The Castellano kid came in Tuesday night. Brought a girl with him—young, pretty, nervous like she'd never been in a place like this before. He showed her around, introduced her to his father. That was her mistake."

"What do you mean?"

"I mean that Carmine Castellano saw something in that girl that reminded him of someone. Or maybe he saw something that he wanted. I don't know what it was, but whatever it was, it was important enough to kill her for."

There it is. The confirmation.

"When?" I ask.

"The girl was alive when she left the Paradise. But not for long after that. Vincent knew it. He tried to stop them from taking her, and they hit him for his trouble. He's alive, probably, but he knows he's dead. That's almost worse."

"Who took her?"

"A man came in Wednesday morning. Older, well-dressed, the kind of man who nobody says no to. He came looking for the girl. Carmine Castellano tried to play it cool, said the girl was gone, didn't know where. The man didn't believe him. By the time the man left, everyone in the Paradise knew that the Castellano family was in trouble. The man's name was Sinclair, though that might not be his real name. He runs a professional operation. Carmine runs a family business. There was no comparison."

"This man," I say. "What did he look like?"

Sylvia gives me a description that matches the man who came to my apartment. Tall, dark-haired, dangerous. She also gives me something else: a detail.

"Sinclair came back the next day with papers. Documents of sale or transfer. He said the Paradise—the bar itself, the liquor license, everything—now belongs to him as compensation for the girl. Carmine Castellano, the owner, didn't even try to fight it. He just signed. That's how much power this man carries. Carmine Castellano, who's been running that bar since 1932, just handed it over without a word because he understood what happened if he said no."
