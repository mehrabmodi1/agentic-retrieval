
The manager, a heavy-set Italian named Russo, is reluctant about the room key. A second twenty makes him philosophical about guest privacy. Room 412 is unchanged from whenever they left. Housekeeper won't clean until someone pays for another night.

The bed was slept in, but there's nothing left behind. Whoever they are, they were careful. The bathroom waste bin is empty. No cigarette butts, no hair clips, nothing. Professional clean, or just lucky. I'm guessing professional.

One detail sticks: There's a faint smell of expensive cologne still hanging in the room. The kind that comes from serious money. Probably imported. The kind of thing that costs more than most people's weekly salary.

I take a towel and sniff it. Yeah. That's the same cologne.

## The Law Office

Mercer & Associates occupies floors three and four of the Whitfield Building on Seventh. I get there around two-thirty in the afternoon. The receptionist is a careful-looking woman named Helen, maybe forty, the kind who maintains a fence between her boss and the world.

She remembers Claire without hesitation. "Miss Hollister was one of our best secretaries. Efficient. Discreet. She hasn't been in since Monday."

Discreet. That word again.

I ask to speak with whoever Claire reported to. Five minutes later I'm sitting across from Thomas Mercer himself, a man of perhaps sixty with white hair and the kind of face that's learned to tell people exactly what they want to hear. He's genuinely distressed about Claire. Or he's a good enough actor that it doesn't matter.

"A troubled girl, in her own way," Mercer tells me, folding his hands on his desk. "Competent at her work, but I sensed she was struggling with something. Personal matters, I assume."

He doesn't know who she was seeing. Didn't ask. Professional distance, he calls it. Another way of saying he didn't want to know.

But here's the thing that bugs me: When I mention the Regency Hotel, something flickers behind Mercer's eyes. Just for a second. A recognition. Then it's gone, buried under another layer of concern.

I mark him down as someone worth talking to again.

## Theories - Day One

**Possibility One:** Simple elopement. Girl meets a man, falls in love, decides to run away with him. Happens. Happens a lot in this city. The Regency Hotel suggests discretion rather than romance—no fancy dinner, no flowers—but maybe they just wanted to be alone. Maybe she's fine. Maybe she's just not coming home.

Hotel manager's records show room 412 was registered to "James H." on the dates in question. Same initial as the mysterious lover in Catherine Halstead's journal—"H." This is likely the connection, though it opens more questions than it answers about how Harvey fits into the Halstead kidnapping.
